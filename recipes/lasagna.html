Lasagna

Description

Lasagna is a classic Italian dish made with layers of pasta and meat.

It is typically made with a rich meat sauce, béchamel sauce, and cheese.

Lasagna is often baked in the oven until golden and bubbly.

It is a popular comfort food and can be made in various styles, including vegetarian versions.

Lasagna is best served hot and can be accompanied by a side salad or garlic bread.

It is a dish that is perfect for family gatherings or special occasions.

Lasagna can be prepared in advance and reheated, making it a convenient option for busy days.

Ingredients

  • Lasagna noodles
  • Ground beef or sausage
  • Onion, chopped
  • Garlic, minced
  • Crushed tomatoes
  • Béchamel sauce (butter, flour, milk)
  • Ricotta cheese
  • Shredded mozzarella cheese
  • Grated Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh basil or parsley for garnish

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Cook the lasagna noodles according to package instructions.
  3. In a skillet, brown the ground beef or sausage with chopped onion and minced garlic.
  4. Add crushed tomatoes to the meat mixture and simmer for 15-20 minutes.
  5. In a separate saucepan, prepare the béchamel sauce by melting butter, adding flour, and whisking in milk until thickened.
  6. In a baking dish, layer cooked noodles, meat sauce, béchamel sauce, ricotta cheese, and mozzarella cheese.
  7. Repeat layers until all ingredients are used, finishing with a layer of béchamel and mozzarella cheese on top.
  8. Sprinkle grated Parmesan cheese over the top.
  9. Bake in the preheated oven for 30-40 minutes or until golden and bubbly.
  10. Let it cool for a few minutes before serving. Garnish with fresh basil or parsley if desired.
